About Me

My name is Steven, I am a George Brown College Graduate and a current Ryerson University student studying Computer Science. I have a strong background in working within a team and always strive for exellence.

My favourite languages to work in are C++ and Java, and learning about game engine architecture is one of my hobbies within programming.

Errant Priest - Global Game Jam (2016)

Errant Priest is a 2D side scrolling adventure game. The player gets "Potential Mana" from killing enemies and does not get to use it until the player performs a ritual at any shrine. The player then gets to chose which spells they wants to bring to the field to combat their enemies.

Hammerfirst (2015)

A game where First-Person Brawling meets Boxercising. Developed in Unity using a Myo Armband as the controller. The player takes control of a descendant of a god to fight creatures that have escaped from their prison. I worked on core gameplay mechanics, such as Punching and Blocking.

Spooky Spelunky - Global Game Jam (2015)

You and another player take control of two miners who have just survived a cave in and must now navigate your way through the labyrinth of mineshafts and fend off horrible creatures. One player takes the responsibility of fighting while the other must hold up the flash light to illuminate the pitch black caves.

BatteryNAssault (2016)

BatteryNAssault is a 3v3 third person shooter arena. It was developed in my last year of college as a group assignment. I mainly worked on the AI portion of the game, but helped out whenever other students needed it. One of the students attempted to get the multiplayer in by the end of the semester but sadly we fell short and could only get some things to replicate.

StevEngine (2017)

This engine is a colaboration between my friend Luc and I. It is created using Open GL 4.2 for rendering, Bullet Physics, and FMOD for sound. It has model loading, 3D Sound, Frustum culling, render batching, and more.
